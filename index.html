A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Kalam was known as the "People's President" due to his popularity and connection with the youth of India. He came from a humble background and overcame several hardships to become one of the most respected figures in the country. He played a pivotal role in India's missile development and nuclear weapons program, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India." Kalam's scientific contributions include his work on India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) and the development of strategic missiles such as Agni and Prithvi. He played a key role in the successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. Beyond his scientific achievements, Kalam was an inspiration to millions of people, especially the youth. He was known for his motivational speeches, emphasizing the importance of education, hard work, and perseverance. Kalam encouraged young people to dream big and strive to achieve their goals, often using himself as an example of someone who rose from humble beginnings to reach great heights. After his presidency, Kalam remained active in various capacities, promoting education, innovation, and social welfare. He was involved in several educational institutions and organizations, working towards providing quality education to all and nurturing scientific temperament among the youth. APJ Abdul Kalam was widely recognized and honored for his contributions. He received numerous awards and accolades, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997. His inspirational life story and his dedication to the progress of the nation continue to inspire generations of Indians.
